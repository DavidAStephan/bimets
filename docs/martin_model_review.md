# A Structural Review of the MARTIN Model: Gaps and Improvement Opportunities Relative to Peer Models

*Internal review prepared for RBA Economic Analysis. Audience: economists who already know MARTIN. All equation references are to `the EViews MARTIN equations.prg` (the EViews source) and `packages/martin/inst/extdata/MARTINMOD_AF.txt` (the bimets file actually solved). Where the two diverge, this is flagged explicitly — it is one of the report's recurring themes.*

---

> **How this review was produced.** This is a multi-agent comparative study commissioned on 2026-06-13. MARTIN was characterised directly from its source (equations.prg, supply_side.prg, solve_model.prg, [MARTINMOD_AF.txt](packages/martin/inst/extdata/MARTINMOD_AF.txt), [equation_catalogue.csv](packages/martin/inst/extdata/equation_catalogue.csv)) and compared, dimension by dimension, against the nine peer-model documents listed in the appendix. Each gap and recommendation was adversarially re-checked against the MARTIN source before inclusion; the yield-curve and ELB-floor fidelity findings in 4.1/4.3 were source-verified by hand.


## 1. Executive summary

MARTIN is the RBA's quarterly, semi-structural, single-equation error-correction workhorse: roughly 95 behavioural ECM equations stitched together with ~80 national-accounts identities, solved deterministically by Gauss-Seidel, fundamentally backward-looking, with adaptive expectations and a labour-only supply side. Its design philosophy is deliberate and, for its core purpose, sound: a transparent, robust, conditional central-tendency forecasting tool whose individual equations track Australian data well, whose behaviour an economist can reason about equation-by-equation, and into which expert judgement enters cleanly as add-factors. It is **not** trying to be a DSGE, and most of the gaps below are not "MARTIN is wrong" but "MARTIN cannot represent mechanism X that a peer can."

The peer set spans the relevant design space: two other Australian models (Treasury's **EMMA**, Outlook Economics' **AUS-M**), the FRB/US-style European semi-structural family (**FR-BDF**, **ECB-BASE**, **ECB-MC**, **ECB-(RE)BASE**), and the RBA's own structural benchmark (the **Rees–Smith–Hall / DINGO–Reno DSGE family**), with the **Mulqueeney–Ballantyne–Hambur (2025) Bulletin** as the in-house comparative reference.

Across eleven modelling dimensions, the same handful of structural threads recur. The most consequential improvement themes:

1. **No forward-looking expectations channel anywhere.** *Why it matters:* MARTIN cannot analyse forward guidance, credible (dis)inflation, anticipated tax/commodity shocks, or expectations de-anchoring — the central macro questions of 2021–2025 — and it systematically understates the speed and front-loading of monetary transmission relative to DINGO.

2. **The inflation anchor PI_E is autonomous and cannot move with policy or realised inflation.** *Why it matters:* the model's entire nominal anchor rests on a hard-coded glide to 2.5%, so it cannot represent credibility loss or re-anchoring — a first-order limitation for an inflation-targeting central bank, and the *cheapest* of all the consequential fixes.

3. **No production function: capital never enters potential output.** *Why it matters:* investment booms/busts (mining capex, an AI/energy-transition capital-deepening wave, a corporate-tax change) have no supply-side footprint, so MARTIN cannot deliver the standard "more capital → higher capacity → disinflationary at given demand" answer that every peer gives.

4. **The fiscal and external sectors have no stock accounting.** *Why it matters:* there is no government debt/deficit, no net-foreign-liability stock, no current-account closure and no debt-elastic exchange-rate premium, so MARTIN is silent on fiscal sustainability and external balance — questions both Australian peers answer natively — and any fiscal or external scenario is a pure demand-shifter that silently assumes costless finance.

5. **The Phillips curve is linear and the slope is frozen at the 2019Q3 sample.** *Why it matters:* a linear, pre-COVID-calibrated curve will under-predict inflation pickups in a hot economy and over-predict disinflation as slack returns — biasing the cash-rate path and the modelled cost of disinflation in exactly the states where policy is most active.

6. **A live reproducibility hazard in the yield curve.** *Why it matters:* the EViews source, the bimets file SIBYL actually solves, **and** SIBYL's own LLM-facing equation catalogue disagree about how N2R/N10R behave, and no regression test pins the yield form — so anyone (or any LLM) reasoning about the term-structure channel from the documentation will mis-predict the model's behaviour.

A guiding observation for everything that follows: a striking share of the highest-value fixes are *architecturally cheap*. Endogenising PI_E, adding a debt-elastic FX premium, bolting on government and external stock-accounting layers, and adding a convex Phillips term are all backward-looking ECM/identity changes that the existing deterministic Gauss-Seidel solver handles natively. The genuinely hard items — forward guidance, anticipated-news dynamics, FX overshoot-on-news, a permanent-income consumption Euler — all require the *same* thing: a forward-looking/stacked-time solver MARTIN does not have. That single architectural fork organises the roadmap in Section 6.

## 2. What MARTIN is today

A self-contained description, so the review stands alone.

**Model class.** Quarterly. ~95 `BEHAVIORAL>` single-equation error-correction equations plus ~80 accounting identities. Built by stitching together individually estimated single equations (consumption, investment, the five export categories, deflators, wages, employment, the household balance sheet, etc.) with national-accounts identities that close real GDP `Y` (L285) and nominal GDP `NY` (L346).

**Solve method.** Deterministic Gauss-Seidel. The EViews source calls `{model}.solve(s=d, d=d, o=g, ...)` (`solve_model.prg` L44/L68); the bimets port uses `bimets::SIMULATE` (`solve_martin.R` L150). There are **no terminal conditions, no stacked-time/perfect-foresight option, and no rational-expectations mode** invoked anywhere. An independent grep of both model files for forward lead operators (`TSLEAD`, `LEAD`, `(+`) returns zero hits: every behavioural equation references only contemporaneous or lagged terms.

**Expectations.** Adaptive only. The single expectations state is inflation expectations `PI_E`, which in the solved bimets file evolves as `PI_E = c1*(0.9*PI_E(-1) + 0.1*2.5)` (MARTINMOD_AF.txt L568–571) — a slow AR(1) (~0.9 persistence, half-life ~7 quarters) anchoring to a **hard-coded 2.5%**. (In the EViews `.prg` the target is the exogenous `pi_target`; in the solved `.txt` it is the literal `2.5`.) `PI_E` enters the wage and price Phillips curves, house prices, every deflator's long-run drift, dwelling investment via the ex-ante real mortgage rate, mortgage credit, the dwelling-stock target, and the live yield equations. It carries **no argument for the cash rate, the output/unemployment gap, or even realised inflation** — so policy and inflation surprises cannot move it within the solve. Offline, `pistar.prg` Kalman-smooths a common factor `cpistar` from survey/bond measures (GBUSEXP, GUNIEXPY, GMAREXPY, GBONYLD) plus realised trimmed-mean inflation, but that is not the in-model recursion.

**Supply side / potential output.** There is **no production function and no role for capital in potential.** Potential *growth* is a pure trend-accounting identity, `TY_POT = TDLLA + TDLLPOP + TDLLHPP` (L42; IDENTITY in MARTINMOD_AF.txt L575–577) = trend labour productivity + trend working-age population + trend average hours. All three are local-linear-trend Kalman state-space trends estimated offline (`supply_side.prg`); in-solve they are deterministic AR(1) glides to exogenous steady states (and trend hours `TDLLHPP` decays to *zero* growth in-solve, mechanically pushing long-run potential toward `TDLLA+TDLLPOP`). Capital stocks accumulate by perpetual inventory — non-mining `KIBN`, mining `KIBRE`, dwellings `KID`, inventories via `KV` — but feed **only** individual investment ECM levels and mining-export supply `XRE` via the single lagged term `LOG(KIBRE(-6))`. They never enter `TY_POT`, and there is no TFP/Solow-residual object. There is no explicit `Y*` level series in the solve; the operative spare-capacity measure is the **unemployment gap** `LURGAP = LUR − TLUR` (L521), reached from real activity only through Okun's law inside the `LUR` equation. NAIRU `TLUR` is a random walk in-model (`tlur = tlur(-1)`), Kalman-estimated offline (`nairu.prg`) following Cusbert (2017).

**Monetary policy.** An inertial Taylor-type rule (MARTINMOD_AF.txt L979–983): `NCR = 0.7*NCR(-1) + 0.3*(RSTAR + ye_TM_infl + (tr_ptm−1)*(ye_TM_infl − PI_TARGET) − tr_lurgap*LURGAP) − (tr_dlur/2)*(LUR−LUR(-2))`, response coefficients **hard-coded** (`tr_ptm = tr_lurgap = tr_dlur = 2`) and imposed via `RESTRICT> c1=1`. It responds to year-ended trimmed-mean inflation, the inflation gap to target, the unemployment gap, and the 2-quarter change in unemployment. The neutral rate `RSTAR` is a slow AR(1) glide to a hard-coded ~0.68% real target, identified offline by a Laubach–Williams state space (`rstar.prg`) and then frozen. **Note two fidelity discrepancies between the EViews `.prg` and the solved `.txt`:** (i) the `.prg` floors the rule at the effective lower bound via `@recode(... > 0.1, ..., 0.1)` (L538); the `.txt` has **no floor** (L981), so the solved cash rate can go negative; and (ii) the term-structure equations diverge (below).

**Transmission.** All reduced-form, single-equation: (1) intertemporal — the real cash rate `RCR` enters the consumption `RC` error-correction term with a small coefficient (`+0.5/100*RCR(-1)`, the *only* direct rate term in consumption); (2) cost-of-capital — `NCR → NBR → RBR →` user cost `IBCR` (which also embeds `N10R` and the exogenous corporate tax rate `IBCTR`) `→` non-mining investment `IBN`; (3) cash-flow/housing — `NCR → NMR → RMR →` house prices `PH`, mortgage credit `NHC`, dwellings `ID`; (4) wealth — via `PH` and financial assets into household net worth `HNW` into `RC`; (5) exchange-rate — `RTWI` error-corrects on the terms of trade and the world real-rate differential `WR2SP`; (6) expectations — weak, because `PI_E` does not respond to policy.

**Term structure (an important correction to the common characterisation).** In the solved bimets file the expectations-hypothesis equations are **live**: `N10R = c1*N10R(-1) + c2*(RSTAR+PI_E+0.25*(NCR−PI_E−RSTAR)) + c3` with `RESTRICT> c1+c2=1` (L102–108), and `N2R` analogously with weight 0.52 and `c2+c3=1` (L111–116). The EViews `.prg` **comments these out** and substitutes random walks `N = N(-1) + d(NCR)` (L545–546/553–554). So SIBYL's yield curve *does* carry forward-looking term-structure content — but the expectations embedded are the adaptive `PI_E` and the cash rate is contemporaneous, so it is "expectations-hypothesis with adaptive expectations," not EH over expected future short rates. The term premium is the constant `c3` plus the pull to `RSTAR+PI_E`; it is **not cyclical or endogenous**.

**Financial sector.** A modelled household balance sheet (the genuine strength): financial assets `NHFA` (an ECM to nominal GNE — a *quantity*, not a valued portfolio), non-financial assets revalued through the dwelling stock × house price, liabilities `NHL`, mortgage credit `NHC`, net worth `NHNW = NHA − NHL`, real wealth `HNW` feeding consumption. The only cyclically sensitive premium is the business spread `NBRSP = c1 + c2*NBRSP(-1) + c3*LURGAP` (a weak reduced-form financial-accelerator proxy keyed on the *aggregate* unemployment gap, not borrower net worth). The mortgage spread `NSP` is ~exogenous AR(1). **No banking sector, no deposit rate, no endogenous credit supply, no leverage/collateral/DSR constraints, no default, no BGG-style accelerator, no equity price.**

**Fiscal sector.** Very thin. Public consumption `GC` and investment `GI` each error-correct to gap-adjusted output `Y/(1−2*LURGAP/100)` (the *only* automatic-stabiliser-like feature, and a spending rule, not a budget identity). `G` enters demand purely through the identities. The corporate tax rate `IBCTR` is exogenous and enters only the cost of capital. Net product taxes `ATS/NATS` are exogenous; GST appears only as 2000 calendar dummies. Household non-labour income `NHOY` is a single ECM lumping transfers, taxes, interest and dividends. **No government budget constraint, no public-debt/deficit stock, no debt-interest feedback, no disaggregated taxes/transfers, no fiscal reaction function, no nominal revenue side at all.**

**External sector.** Disaggregated export volumes — mining `XRE` (on lagged mining capital), manufactures `XM`, services `XS`, agriculture `XAG`, other `XO` (on world GDP `WY` and the real export-weighted index `REWI`). Imports `M` via import-adjusted demand `IAD` (a Cobb-Douglas of demand components by import intensity) and relative import prices. Terms of trade `TOT = PX/PM`. The real `RTWI` is a backward ECM on `TOT` and `WR2SP` — a real-rate-differential reduced form, **not forward UIP**. **No current-account/net-foreign-liability stock, no balance-of-payments closure, no net foreign income flow, no valuation effects.**

**Labour market.** Employment `LE` is a dynamic labour-demand ECM (on `Y`, real labour cost `RLC`, productivity/hours trends), estimated by TSLS — a *reduced-form* factor demand, not a marginal-product FOC, and purely backward-looking (no labour hoarding). Unemployment `LUR` via Okun's law. The labour force `LF = LE/(1−LUR/100)` is a residual identity; participation `LPR` is a **reported aggregate with no feedback**; hours enter only as the exogenous trend `TDLLHPP`. **No search-and-matching, no vacancies/Beveridge curve, no gross flows, no endogenous participation, no cyclical hours, no duration/long-term-unemployment cohorts, no worker heterogeneity, no wage-bargaining microstructure** (wages `PW` are a reduced-form Phillips curve).

**Prices & wages.** Trimmed-mean inflation `PTM` is a hybrid gap-plus-marginal-cost Phillips curve: the error-correction term is a markup of prices over a weighted average of Balassa-Samuelson-adjusted unit labour cost `NULCBS` and consumer-import price `PMCG`; the cyclical term is a **single linear** `c7*LURGAP`. Wages `PW` are a separate Phillips curve in `LURGAP/LUR` (a mild denominator scaling, not convexity), `D(LUR)`, the adaptive `PI_E`, and lagged GDP-deflator inflation. Both impose a unit long-run (vertical) restriction. A wage-price spiral exists (`PW → PAE → NHCOE → NULC → NULCBS → PTM`) but is anchored by the slow exogenous `PI_E` and a long 8-quarter-averaged price-feedback lag, so within-solve second-round dynamics are muted. Exchange-rate pass-through is explicit and multi-stage (`PM` on world export prices, oil, and `NTWI`; then `PM → PMCG → PTM`). A large deflator cascade (`PC`, `PG`, `PID`, `PIBN`, `PIBRE`, `PM`, `PMCG`, the export deflators) closes each series to `PTM/PC/PM`/world prices with hard-coded unit-cointegration restrictions. Headline `P` = trimmed mean + contemporaneous and lagged oil terms — so **oil reaches core only slowly, via the lagged `PMCG` level in the `PTM` error-correction term**.

**Stock-flow consistency.** *Partial and asymmetric.* Capital stocks and the household balance sheet accumulate (and the household *real-asset* side has a genuine housing-valuation channel into consumption). But household *financial* wealth `NHFA` is an ECM to nominal income rather than an accumulated saving-plus-revaluation stock — and the computed household saving flow `NHS` feeds **nothing downstream** (a dead end), so saving and wealth are disconnected. There is no government-debt stock, no net-foreign-asset stock, no corporate or banking balance sheet, **no income/production side of GDP at all** (nominal GDP is built only from the expenditure side — the root reason a corporate sector cannot be constructed), and **no Godley-style adding-up constraint** that sector net-lending positions sum to zero.

**Estimation.** Single-equation, classical (frequentist), equation-by-equation — **not** system FIML, **not** Bayesian for the behavioural block. 42 equations are OLS with HAC SEs, one (`NSP`) White, exactly one (`LE`) TSLS. Identification is by **theory-imposed restrictions** (51 `c1=1` homogeneity cards plus ~52 genuine cross-coefficient restrictions; a literal `(1-1)*LOG(...)` device to pin deflator pass-through to 1), not instruments or sign restrictions. Every behavioural `TSRANGE` ends 2019Q3; the SIBYL default is `coefficients="frozen"` (re-fit free coefficients on the embedded 2019Q3 sample, reproducing published values), with re-estimation across the COVID break an explicit opt-in. The offline trends (`TLUR`, `RSTAR`, `TDLLA/POP/HPP`, `PI_E`) are quasi-Bayesian Kalman state spaces with informative priors calibrated to published RBA values — but their estimation uncertainty is discarded at the offline/online boundary, and structural breaks are absorbed by hand-placed dummies rather than recursive/CUSUM testing.

**Uncertainty / shocks.** Deterministic solve only. The native `run_sims.prg` is a one-at-a-time +1% perturbation IRF generator (faithfully ported in the package as the tested `sensitivity_matrix()`). **No structural-shock identification, no historical decomposition, no fan charts, no analytic multiplier matrix in use.** SIBYL adds an opt-in `solve_martin_stochastic` that draws independent normal disturbances at each equation's regression SE over the forecast tail — a disturbance band that ignores parameter and cross-equation-correlation uncertainty.

## 3. The peer models

**EMMA — Treasury Macroeconometric Model of Australia v1.0** (Bullen et al., 2021). A "3G" structural macroeconometric model: ~20 key behavioural ECMs plus a large national-accounts and flow-of-funds identity system, three industries plus a housing quasi-industry, single representative household, single consolidated government. *Best-in-class for, and the single most directly relevant comparator on:* the **fiscal block** (full government budget constraint `G + (r−g)B = T`, five disaggregated tax bases as effective-rate-times-base identities, an explicit calibrated debt-stabilising rule with personal income tax as swing instrument) and **stock-flow consistency** (a four-agent flow-of-funds matrix where net lending sums to zero every period). Also carries an explicit per-industry **CES production function** (capital genuinely in potential), hybrid expectations with a genuine forward-looking financial block (forward UIP, EH term structure), Tobin's-q investment, and an optional open-loop optimal-control monetary policy. Methodologically the closest twin to MARTIN: single-equation maximum likelihood, classical not Bayesian.

**AUS-M — Outlook Economics** (descendant of Treasury's TRYM / the Murphy/AMPS lineage). A large-scale Keynes–Klein structural model with sticky prices, demand-determined short-run output, an input-output core and AIDS demand systems. *Best-in-class for:* the **external sector** (full balance-of-payments stock-flow accounting — every borrowing flow has a matching stock, foreign-currency stocks carry FX valuation effects, the current account accumulates into net foreign liabilities `VNFLZ` that feed back into the FX risk premium and consumption), the **fiscal block** (PSBR identity, accumulated net-debt stock, debt interest on the stock, a fiscal reaction function on the labour-tax rate), and the **richest labour market** of any peer (a Beveridge curve with an explicit vacancy stock, a 7-cohort long-term-unemployment model driving an endogenous two-component NAIRU, cyclical hours, demographically disaggregated participation). Also has a forward-looking financial sector (real TWI with a +40-quarter lead) and industry-by-industry CES production. Classical, single-equation with extensive joint/systems estimation; not Bayesian/FIML.

**FR-BDF — Banque de France** (WP #736, 2019; re-estimated WP #1044, 2026). A ~400-equation FRB/US-style semi-structural model. *Best-in-class for:* the **switchable expectations** architecture (every PAC equation carries an expected-present-value term filled by either a Bayesian VAR satellite "E-SAT," full model-consistent expectations, or a hybrid), a **rich endogenous financial block** with a genuine BGG-style accelerator (corporate spreads on net market leverage `D/E`), a micro-founded **CES supply side** (capital in potential), forward UIP, and a **full SFC structure** across five institutional sectors with explicit revaluation and a debt-stabilising transfers rule. The most architecturally transferable exemplar because it is semi-structural ECM-style, and its 2026 update demonstrates the discipline of **re-estimating Phillips slopes through the COVID/energy episode** (wage slope flatter 0.53→0.46, price slope steeper).

**ECB-BASE** (Angelini et al., WP 2315, 2019). The ECB's large-scale estimated semi-structural euro-area model, explicitly modelled on FRB/US and Bank of Canada's LENS. *Best-in-class for:* the **WAPRO** Bayesian Kalman-filtered sub-model that **jointly identifies the output gap and NAIRU** with the wage/price Phillips curves and exports model-consistent inflation equations into the main model; a switchable VAR-vs-rational expectations engine; a full **Cobb-Douglas production function** with capital in potential; a rich endogenous financial block (EH term structure + endogenous term premium + six lending spreads including a **deposit rate** + valued household wealth with a **revaluation term**); a genuine **fiscal block** with an accumulating debt stock; an **NFA stock** that feeds household net interest income and consumption; and a **principled, statistically grounded add-factor framework** (a state-space unobserved-component residual model with estimated persistence) in place of MARTIN's mechanical `*-0.5` decay. Also supports stochastic simulation and fan charts.

**ECB-MC — ECB Multi-Country Model** (Angelini et al., WP 3119). A country-disaggregated sibling of ECB-BASE covering the five largest euro-area economies, used in the Eurosystem Projection Exercises. *Best-in-class for:* **forward-looking endogenous country risk premia** (spreads widen with the *expected* output gap and the fiscal position), the **Basic Model Elasticities** framework of standardised, policy-invariant shocks, and a **formal forecast decomposition** (ΔForecast = Impact-of-Assumptions + Statistical-Carry-over + Impact-of-History + Implied-Judgement) that quantifies the judgement content of each round. Two-agent (rule-of-thumb vs optimising) heterogeneity gives non-Ricardian fiscal multipliers. Bayesian throughout, with ECB-BASE estimates as priors; documents real-time "splicing" of residuals across data revisions.

**ECB-(RE)BASE** (Adjemian et al., WP 2965, 2024). The model-consistent/rational-expectations variant of ECB-BASE, re-solving the same system under VAR-based, fully rational, or hybrid expectations via Dynare perfect-foresight/extended-path solvers. *Best-in-class for:* demonstrating a **switchable forward-looking solve** on a semi-structural model, **anticipated/news shocks** (announce at *t*, materialise at *t*+20), an explicit **unconventional-policy channel** (asset purchases acting on the term premium plus an augmented-UIP FX leg), a forward-looking financial block (console-bond pricing, dividend-discount equities, wealth revalued by realised returns), and a permanent-income consumption specification that **avoids the forward-guidance puzzle**.

**RBA DSGE family — Rees–Smith–Hall (RDP 2015-07) + DSGE Reno (Gibbs–Hambur–Nodari, RDP 2018-04)** (the parent of DINGO). The RBA's own micro-founded, rational-expectations, Bayesian-estimated small-open-economy multi-sector DSGE. *Best-in-class for, and the sharpest in-house contrast on:* **fully model-consistent expectations** (every Euler, Q, Phillips-curve, term-structure and UIP equation contains explicit `E_t` of future variables), **capital genuinely in sectoral production**, a **forward-UIP exchange rate tied to an accumulating NFA stock** with a debt-elastic risk premium, **~20–30 identified structural shocks** with variance and historical decompositions, and **anticipated (news) shock** analysis. Crucially honest caveats apply: it has **no unemployment/NAIRU object at all** (the gap is an output-deviation concept, a *different* operational philosophy, not strictly "more advanced" for the RBA's unemployment-centric communication), and its financial sector is its own *weakest* dimension.

**Mulqueeney–Ballantyne–Hambur (RBA Bulletin, April 2025).** Not a model but the authoritative in-house comparison of MARTIN, DINGO and Murphy's hybrid, decomposing transmission into four channels (exchange rate; asset prices/wealth; savings/investment; cash flow). *Best for context:* it documents that the exchange-rate channel is "particularly important in MARTIN" (one-third to two-thirds of the inflation response), that MARTIN's inertial rule drives the cash rate *below* baseline after a hike (which is partly why MARTIN shows a small, transient inflation response), that the cash-flow channel is small in aggregate, and — importantly — it explicitly frames MARTIN's adaptive-expectations, no-credit-channel design as a **deliberate, defensible robustness/transparency choice**, not merely a deficiency. Its normative message — *model diversity as the uncertainty device* — is itself a stance worth adopting.

## 4. Dimension-by-dimension assessment

Each subsection states how MARTIN does it, how the best peers do it, the *verified* gaps (with severity), and concrete recommendations (exemplar, effort, payoff, feasibility). The single most important honesty constraint, applied throughout: where a fix requires forward-looking expectations or a forward solver, it is flagged as architecturally out of reach for the current Gauss-Seidel core, and a backward-compatible surrogate is offered where one exists.

---

### 4.1 Expectations formation

**MARTIN.** Purely adaptive/backward-looking. The one expectations state, `PI_E`, is an autonomous AR(1) glide to 2.5%; there is no model-consistent channel and no forward solve. The yield curve carries the *only* genuine forward structure (the live EH equations), but on adaptive expectations and a contemporaneous cash rate.

**Best peers.** FR-BDF, ECB-BASE/MC/(RE)BASE all carry switchable VAR-based / model-consistent / hybrid expectations over a common structure; the RBA DSGE and DINGO are fully model-consistent. EMMA and AUS-M target forward-lookingness precisely at the term structure and FX (forward UIP, model-consistent long-run inflation read off the model's own future price level) — the most directly transferable Australian template.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No model-consistent / forward-looking solve anywhere** *(high)*. The future path of policy, income or inflation cannot affect today except through lagged states. This is the single defining contrast with every peer and the root of the "MARTIN passes policy through too slowly" finding versus DINGO.
- **Inflation expectations are autonomous** *(high)*. `PI_E` has no argument for the rate, the gap, or realised inflation, so de-anchoring/re-anchoring — the central question of 2021–24 — cannot be represented. (Sharpened by the verifier: in the *solved* `.txt` the target is the literal `2.5`, so even a regime-change scenario already expressible via `pi_target` in the `.prg` is **not** expressible in the file SIBYL runs.)
- **Exchange rate is a backward real-rate-differential reduced form, not forward UIP** *(medium)*. The AUD cannot jump on news about future differentials. Partially defensible — the ECM does capture the steady-state comparative static.
- **Consumption and investment have no expected-future-income / permanent-income channel** *(medium)*. `RC` is an ECM in current income/wealth plus the lagged real cash rate; investment uses lagged cost-of-capital levels, not forward Tobin's-Q. Partially defensible for robust unconditional forecasting — EMMA deliberately keeps C/I static for the same reason.
- **The one piece of forward structure that EXISTS (the EH yield curve) is undocumented and inconsistent across artefacts** *(medium, and the most operationally dangerous)*. The bimets file runs the live EH equations; the EViews `.prg` random-walks them; **and SIBYL's own LLM-facing `equation_catalogue.csv` (L55–56) describes N2R/N10R as "Simple expectations rule: drifts with the cash rate"** — the *wrong* (EViews) form, which is the artefact the judgement layer reads to propose add-factors on yields. No regression test pins the yield form (the `HEADLINE` set in `test-regression-against-bimets.R` is `Y/RC/GNE/LUR/PTM/NCR` only). The hazard is three-way and live.
- **No anticipated (news) shock capability** *(medium)*. A corollary of the no-forward-solve gap; a future-dated add-factor only bites in the quarter it lands.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* the wage block is *doubly* backward (adaptive `PI_E` plus an 8-quarter trailing GDP-deflator term), which is the precise mechanism behind "understates the persistence of an inflation shock"; and world/foreign expectations (`WY`, `WP`, `WPCOM`, `WR2R`) are also fully adaptive, so anticipated foreign paths cannot move the AUD even in principle.

**Recommendations.**

Replace the literal `2.5` with `PI_TARGET`; expose `ρ, ω` as fixed `options()` constants (not `ESTIMATE`-fitted) so `ω=0` reproduces today's frozen path bit-stably. | ECB-BASE; EMMA | small | high | fits current ECM |
| E2 | **Reconcile and document the live EH yield curve** (Step 1: fix CLAUDE.md, the ground-truth note, **and** the `equation_catalogue.csv` descriptions; add a regression pin on N2R/N10R). ### 4.2 Supply side & potential output

**MARTIN.** Labour-only trend-accounting potential; no production function, no capital in potential, no TFP. The output gap is the unemployment gap, reached via a single Okun equation with a time-varying coefficient.

**Best peers.** EMMA and AUS-M (per-industry CES with capital and a fixed factor; capital in the BGP), FR-BDF (CES with factor-price frontier), ECB-BASE/MC (Cobb-Douglas potential `y* = a* + αk* + (1−α)(...)` with capital, and WAPRO jointly identifying the gap and NAIRU). The RBA DSGE has capital in production but, deliberately, *no* unemployment/NAIRU — a different philosophy. The Bulletin's own supply-demand framing confirms MARTIN's reduced-form capacity-gap mechanism matches how the RBA communicates.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No production function: capital accumulation cannot affect potential** *(high)*. A mining-capex boom/bust, a corporate-tax change, or an AI capital-deepening wave has no supply-side footprint, so MARTIN under-states the disinflationary supply response to investment booms.
- **No TFP / Solow-residual concept and no capital-deepening channel** *(high)*. Trend productivity is a black-box univariate trend reverting to an exogenous scalar; the durable-vs-transient productivity question central to the current Australian debate cannot be answered internally.
- **No explicit `Y*` object; the unemployment gap is the sole inflation/policy gap** *(high)*. All capacity pressure funnels through one Okun equation; if the Okun relationship shifts (labour hoarding, the post-COVID tightness-without-output episode), the inflation signal degrades with no independent cross-check. *(Verifier qualifier: MARTIN does construct an output gap and Okun mapping **offline** in `rstar.prg` — the gap is the absence of an **in-solve, capital-based** `Y*` cross-check, and that offline series is a free reference for the recommended diagnostic.)*
- **No balanced-growth-path closure tying factor demands to a steady state** *(medium)*. Investment error-corrects to demand aggregates plus a user-cost term, not a marginal-product condition; the capital-output ratio can drift in long simulations. Lower severity given MARTIN's 2–3 year horizon.
- **Mining-export "capital" link is a single reduced-form ECM term, not a supply technology** *(medium)*. `XRE` on `LOG(KIBRE(-6))` is a fixed-elasticity lagged-capital term — the catalogue's "drives XRE through the production function" is loose marketing; there is demonstrably no production function.
- **NAIRU and trends are exogenous-in-solve random walks set offline** *(medium)*. They cannot re-identify jointly with the realised inflation-unemployment configuration within a round, unlike WAPRO. The offline Kalman methodology is sound; the gap is the lack of in-round joint identification.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **no capacity-utilisation / capital-services margin** at all — even fixed capital cannot be worked harder or idled, so the cyclical part of measured TFP routes entirely through the Okun margin; (ii) the supply and demand sides are estimated on **three internally inconsistent capacity objects** (the NAIRU in `nairu.prg`, the ygap in `rstar.prg`, and the in-solve `LOKLAG/TY` from `supply_side.prg`) with no guarantee they cohere — arguably as important as "no production function"; (iii) trend hours `TDLLHPP` **decays to zero growth in-solve**, mechanically forcing long-run potential toward `TDLLA+TDLLPOP`; (iv) **no land/housing fixed factor** despite housing being a first-class sector; (v) **no participation-trend / effective-labour-quality** channel in potential (`TDLLPOP` is raw working-age population).

**Recommendations.**

| # | Change | Exemplar | Effort | Payoff | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 | **Add a Cobb-Douglas potential-output identity** `LYSTAR = a_tfp + α*LOG(KSTAR) + (1−α)*L_EFF`, with `KSTAR` from existing stocks and trend TFP backed out as a Solow residual; keep the labour-augmenting trend so the BGP is Uzawa-consistent. | ECB-MC eq.33; EMMA | large | high | needs new data (`KSTAR` needs a utilisation/efficiency assumption; reconcile `LYSTAR` with the existing growth-potential `TY_POT`); note re-estimation collides with the frozen default |
| S2 | **Introduce `YGAP = (LOG(Y) − LYSTAR)*100` as a parallel diagnostic / blended gap**, *without* initially disturbing the estimated `PTM/PW/NCR`. Reuse the offline `rstar.prg` ygap as a ready cross-check series. | ECB-BASE/MC; FR-BDF | medium | high | fits current ECM |
| S3 | **Re-anchor non-mining investment `IBN` to a production-function FOC target** (pin the capital-output ratio to a BGP); keep existing short-run dynamics and estimation. | FR-BDF; AUS-M | large | moderate | needs system re-estimation (collides with frozen default) |
| S4 | **Give the resource sector a capacity/utilisation supply block** for `XRE` (CES with a fixed resource factor + utilisation). | EMMA; AUS-M | medium | moderate | **needs new data** (fixed-resource-factor series + utilisation benchmark + re-estimation) |
| S5 | **Decompose trend productivity `TDLLA`** into labour-augmenting technical change vs capital deepening in the offline state-space step; feed the labour-augmenting trend into `LYSTAR` and the BGP scalar. (Note: identification quietly imports a production/FOC relationship into the offline filter.) | EMMA `λ_N`; FR-BDF | medium | moderate | needs new data |
| S6 | **If a full production function is out of scope: document the exclusion and ship a calibrated `YGAP` capacity-gap diagnostic** next to `LURGAP` each round, flagging Okun drift. The best effort-adjusted move; pairs with S2. | ECB-BASE; the Bulletin's capacity framing | small | incremental | fits current ECM |

*Where MARTIN's simplicity is defensible:* the Bulletin's own supply-demand framing — and the RBA's unemployment-centric communication — make a reduced-form capacity gap a legitimate choice for a 2–3 year operational model. The honest minimum is **S6** (diagnostic + transparency), not a structural rebuild.

### 4.3 Monetary policy & interest-rate transmission

**MARTIN.** An inertial Taylor rule with hard-coded coefficients, transmitting through reduced-form rate identities and ECM activity equations. Live EH yields but on adaptive expectations; a constant term premium; an exogenous, domestically-calibrated neutral rate.

**Best peers.** ECB-BASE/MC (EH term structure + endogenous counter-cyclical term premium + six lending rates including a deposit rate); ECB-(RE)BASE (unconventional/QE channel via the term premium and augmented UIP); EMMA (neutral rate world-anchored via UIP+PPP; open-loop optimal control); AUS-M (neutral rate error-corrects to the world real long rate; genuine EH long bond); RBA DSGE (neutral rate pinned structurally; EH Euler). The Bulletin documents stage-1 pass-through as ~one-to-one across models and explains MARTIN's small inflation response via its rule overshooting the cash rate downward.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No genuinely forward-looking yield curve** *(high)*. Even with live EH equations, long rates respond only to the contemporaneous stance, not an expected future path (no forward solve). A "higher-for-longer" signal is essentially invisible unless the current `NCR` is moved. *Meaningfully weaker than the common "MARTIN uses random-walk yields" claim, which is true only of the EViews `.prg`.*
- **Term premium is constant, not endogenous/counter-cyclical** *(medium)*. No widening in downturns, no global-term-premium spillover (e.g. US long-rate moves), despite `WR2R` existing in the model but not being wired into domestic yields.
- **Neutral rate `RSTAR` is fully exogenous and domestically calibrated** *(medium)*. A shift in the global neutral rate — a central 2024–26 question — moves `RSTAR` only by manual re-calibration.
- **Expectations channel of monetary policy is essentially absent** *(high)*. A tightening cannot re-anchor expectations; combined with the rule's downward overshoot, this is the structural reason MARTIN shows a small, transient inflation response.
- **Lending-rate block is a single spread on the cash rate** *(medium)*. No deposit rate, no funding-cost structure, no short/long blend; pass-through is mechanically one-to-one, missing incomplete/asymmetric pass-through.
- **No unconventional-policy / balance-sheet / QE channel** *(medium)*. The 2020–21 bond-purchase and yield-target episode can only be hand-imposed via add-factors on `N10R`.
- **Exchange-rate channel is a real-rate-differential reduced form, not forward UIP** *(low)*. The DSGE's `φ_q≈0` suggests the FX channel is empirically modest for Australia.
- **No optimal-control / loss-minimising policy option** *(low)*. Only the fixed rule runs; arguably outside MARTIN's intended role.
- **FIDELITY DEFECT: the bimets Taylor rule drops the EViews ELB floor** *(medium)*. `equations.prg` L538 floors `NCR` at 0.1; MARTINMOD_AF.txt L981 has no floor, so the solved cash rate can go negative. Symmetric with the yield divergence (the `.txt` *restores* the EH yields the `.prg` commented out, but *drops* the floor the `.prg` added).
- **No credit-quantity / bank-lending channel** *(low)*. A known, defensible scope choice — the RBA maintains the Brassil–Major–Rickards (2022) banking-augmented MARTIN separately.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **no endogenous equity/share-price wealth channel** — the wealth leg runs only through house prices and `NHFA` (which is an ECM to nominal GNE, *not* rate-sensitive), so the rate → equity-valuation → wealth → consumption leg is entirely missing; (ii) the consumption intertemporal term uses the **ex-post** real cash rate `RCR` even though an ex-ante `RMRE` exists, so a credibly-signalled inflation path does not move the real rate households face; (iii) the direct intertemporal channel is **very small** (coefficient `0.5/100`) and is the *only* direct rate term in consumption; (iv) **no bank-funding-cost shock lever** (NSP/NBRSP cannot be widened independently of the cash rate and `LURGAP`).

**Recommendations.**

| # | Change | Exemplar | Effort | Payoff | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| M1 | **Reconcile the ELB floor and the EViews/bimets term-structure discrepancy first** (re-add the `@recode` floor wrapping the *inner* expression of `NCR`; document the live EH yields and fix the catalogue's "drifts with the cash rate" text). | M3 | **World-anchor `RSTAR`** — but **only after** making `WRR` (or a new world-neutral-rate input) a live exogenous path, *then* error-correcting `RSTAR` to it. As written against the current frozen-`WRR`-glide-to-(-1), it merely relocates the manual-calibration knob; **payoff downgraded to moderate** unless the world-rate input is also upgraded. *(This is the one recommendation the verifier flagged as not well-founded as originally specified.)* | EMMA; AUS-M | small | moderate | fits current ECM |

### 4.4 Financial sector & frictions

**MARTIN.** A thin, reduced-form block: a genuine modelled household balance sheet plus two spreads (one weakly endogenous, one near-exogenous) and two endogenous asset prices (housing, commodities). No banking sector.

**Best peers.** FR-BDF (BGG accelerator on corporate leverage; mortgage-debt stock with a DSR stress indicator; full sectoral net-asset positions), ECB-BASE/MC (six lending rates including a deposit rate; valued household portfolio with a revaluation term; forward-looking endogenous risk premia), ECB-(RE)BASE (forward console-bond and dividend-discount pricing; wealth revalued by realised returns), AUS-M (All-Ordinaries index feeding a full valued-wealth aggregate `VMZ`; rich house-price ECM). The RBA DSGE is a wash with MARTIN here (its own weakest dimension). The Bulletin and **Brassil–Major–Rickards (2022) "MARTIN Gets a Bank Account"** are the directly portable in-house exemplars.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No banking sector and no endogenous credit SUPPLY** *(high)*. `NHC`/`NHL` are pure demand-side ECMs; the only lender-side object is the exogenous `NSP`. A credit-standards/macroprudential tightening (GFC; the 2015–17 APRA investor/interest-only measures) can only enter as an exogenous add-factor, and demand- vs supply-driven credit slowdowns cannot be distinguished. **The single largest financial-sector limitation.**
- **No financial accelerator / balance-sheet channel keyed on borrower net worth** *(high)*. `NBRSP` keys on the *aggregate* unemployment gap, not firm/household leverage; there is no external-finance-premium-on-leverage loop. MARTIN under-states the non-linear amplification of large adverse shocks and the state-dependence of policy that Brassil et al. demonstrate is material.
- **Spreads and risk premia are reduced-form or exogenous, with no endogenous term/credit premium** *(medium)*. *(Verifier wording correction: the bimets bond equations hard-code the internal cash-rate-gap weights (0.52/0.25) but estimate the persistence/level coefficients under adding-up restrictions — the accurate statement is "no time-varying or endogenous term premium," not "all coefficients fixed.")*
- **Financial (non-housing) wealth is a quantity ECM, not a valued portfolio — no equity-price wealth effect** *(medium)*. A 2020-style equity crash/rebound or an AI-driven equity boom produces no direct consumption response. Less severe than the credit-supply gap given housing dominates Australian wealth, but a clean omission relative to every peer except the RBA DSGE.
- **No leverage / collateral / debt-service-ratio constraints** *(medium)*. MARTIN cannot capture the heightened rate-sensitivity of households when DSRs are elevated (directly relevant to the 2022–24 tightening into a high-debt economy) — the heterogeneity the RBA's HANK agenda targets.
- **No default / NPL block** *(low)*. No peer models default structurally either, so MARTIN is not an outlier; matters only if repurposed for tail-risk/financial-stability work.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **no bank funding-cost / pass-through stage** (no deposit rate, no wholesale/BBSW rate) — distinct from "spreads are reduced-form" because it concerns the *absence of a funding-cost stage entirely*; (ii) **no saver/deposit-rate cash-flow channel** — only the borrower side feeds consumption, biasing the aggregate response given Australia's large deposit base; (iii) **no money/credit aggregate** as a financial-cycle indicator; (iv) **no financial-conditions-index / risk-appetite state** (a global risk-off episode can only be hand-imposed); (v) the housing-wealth channel MARTIN *does* have rests on a **unit-elasticity, no-valuation `NHNFA` identity** that is itself a strong simplification.

**Recommendations.**

| # | Change | Exemplar | Effort | Payoff | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| F1 | **Make `NBRSP` a leverage-sensitive external-finance premium** (light-touch BGG): add `c4*LEV_B(-1)`. A *true* closed accelerator requires endogenising the leverage proxy (a business-debt stock plus a valuation of business capital), not just a lagged regressor. | FR-BDF `s = κ0 + κ1*(D/E) + AR(1)` | medium | high | needs new data |
| F2 | **Add a cyclically-sensitive mortgage spread and a household DSR channel** (`NSP` on `LURGAP` and `DSR(-1)`; `DSR` into `NHC` demand and optionally an `RC` interaction). The DSR channel into NSP/NHC needs **no new data** (`NMR`, `NHL`, `NHDY` are in-model); the optional `RC` interaction requires re-fitting consumption. | FR-BDF DSR; ECB-MC mortgage-minus-deposit spread | medium | high | fits current ECM |

### 4.5 Fiscal sector

**MARTIN.** No fiscal *sector*; a fiscal *demand wedge*. Two real-spending ECMs closing to gap-adjusted output, an exogenous corporate tax rate, exogenous product taxes, and a single lumped household non-labour-income ECM. No nominal-side budget.

**Best peers.** EMMA (full budget constraint, five tax bases, calibrated debt-stabilising rule) and AUS-M (PSBR identity, accumulated debt stocks, disaggregated effective-rate taxes including GST, a fiscal reaction function) are the directly comparable Australian benchmarks. FR-BDF and ECB-BASE carry budget constraints with accumulating debt and a transfers/debt rule; ECB-MC runs its fiscal block exogenous *for forecasting* (a legitimate division of labour). The RBA DSGE is deliberately Ricardian/minimal — *not* more advanced in coverage, but more internally consistent.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No government budget constraint and no public debt/deficit stock** *(high)*. A stimulus add-factored onto `GC/GI` raises `Y` with zero debt consequence; MARTIN cannot represent the support-now-vs-sustainability-later trade-off.
- **No fiscal reaction function / debt-stabilising rule** *(high)*. Nothing returns the fiscal position to a sustainable path; multipliers are unconditioned on the financing response (biased to the high/most-Keynesian end). FR-BDF shows the rule switches Ricardian crowding-out on/off.
- **No disaggregated taxes or transfers; household fiscal wedge buried in `NHOY`** *(high)*. No effective-rate-times-base structure → no revenue-side automatic stabiliser; a tax/transfer/Stage-3-style reform can only be an opaque `NHOY` add-factor; like the RSH DSGE, MARTIN papers over the 2000 GST with dummies rather than mechanism.
- **Automatic stabilisers are only a partial, spending-side device** *(medium)*. The `LURGAP` gross-up in `GC/GI` is the sole stabiliser; the largest Australian stabilisers (progressive income tax, unemployment transfers) are absent, and the model cannot decompose discretionary vs automatic fiscal.
- **No explicit monetary-fiscal interaction or debt-interest feedback** *(medium)*. Monetary tightening cannot raise debt-service costs; the wiring is partly present (`NCR` already drives `N10R`) and unused.
- **No explicit fiscal-multiplier object** *(medium)*. The multiplier is implicit, unconditioned and recoverable only by add-factoring `GC/GI`.
- **Public investment does not build a public capital stock or feed supply** *(low)*. A symptom of the broader no-production-function design; honestly cross-cutting with the supply dimension.
- **Defensible-by-design caveat** *(low)*. Treating government demand as quasi-exogenous and Treasury-informed is legitimate (ECB-MC does likewise). The sharper criticism is narrow: the *half-measure* — an endogenous spending stabiliser with no financing counterpart — is arguably the **least coherent** point on the spectrum, less clean than either a Ricardian closure or a full budget block. This right-sizes the recommendation toward a minimal switchable layer, not a wholesale re-architecture.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) the public-demand deflator `PG` is structurally entangled with inventory and import-intensity pricing and there is **no separate government-consumption vs -investment deflator**; (ii) **no Commonwealth/state or general-government/public-corporations split** (`G` is a single consolidated aggregate); (iii) GST's **price-level pass-through** is unavailable except via ad hoc `PC/PID/PIBN` add-factors; (iv) a debt-interest layer would be **domestically closed only**, because there is no net-foreign-income flow for the externally-held debt portion.

**Recommendations.**

| # | Change | Exemplar | Effort | Payoff | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| G1 | **Add a minimal budget-and-debt accounting layer** as pure recursive identities: nominal revenue `NREV` (effective-rate × base, reusing in-model series), net lending `NLEND`, debt stock `BG = BG(-1) − NLEND + valuation`, debt interest `INT = iir*BG(-1)` reusing `N10R`. Report `DEFICIT/GDP` and `BG/GDP`. Re-baseline against the bimets fixture; calibrate rates to reproduce historical `NATS` in-sample. *(Note: MARTIN has no disaggregated tax/transfer series and no income side, so this is also partly needs-new-data.)* | EMMA; AUS-M `BPSBRZ`; ECB-BASE `go_b9n`/`go_mal` | medium (large w/ data) | high | fits current ECM (Gauss-Seidel handles downstream identities) |

### 4.6 External sector & balance of payments

**MARTIN.** Disaggregated trade volumes, a reduced-form real-TWI ECM, and a coherent commodity-price → investment → exports → ToT → FX chain — but **no current-account/balance-of-payments accounting** of any kind.

**Best peers.** AUS-M (the strongest and closest: full BoP stock-flow accounting with valuation effects; NFL feeds the FX risk premium and consumption), EMMA (forward UIP; explicit external-balance closure), ECB-BASE/MC (an accumulating NFA stock feeding net interest income and consumption — the most directly transplantable semi-structural construction), FR-BDF (forward UIP; energy/non-energy import split), RBA DSGE (forward UIP tied to an NFA stock with a debt-elastic premium). The Bulletin confirms the FX channel is "particularly important in MARTIN" and that no NFA-stock feedback exists.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No accumulating net-foreign-asset/liability stock and no balance-of-payments closure** *(high)*. Net trade enters only the GDP/income identities; net exports can drift inconsistently with any financing path. *The single largest structural gap on this dimension.* Severity high rather than critical only because MARTIN's mandate is the inflation/output forecast.
- **No net foreign (primary) income flow channel** *(medium)*. The primary-income deficit is the dominant component of Australia's current account and the main GDP-vs-GNI wedge; MARTIN misses a real-income channel into consumption.
- **No external stock-feedback onto the exchange rate (no debt-elastic risk premium)** *(medium)*. A persistent CAD does not gradually weigh on the AUD — the RBA's *own* DSGE closes exactly this loop that MARTIN leaves open; the long-run FX level is pinned almost entirely by the ToT.
- **Reduced-form (non-forward-looking) exchange rate rather than UIP** *(medium)*. Honestly *partly a design-class limitation* — a forward UIP cannot be bolted onto a backward Gauss-Seidel solve. The reduced form tracks the ToT well.
- **Imports not split energy/non-energy; single relative-price elasticity** *(medium)*. An oil-price shock and a manufactured-goods shock are forced through the same elasticity, mis-estimating pass-through; may partly explain the Bulletin's "imports rise after a hike" oddity.
- **No valuation effects on any external position** *(low)*. Australia's "natural hedge" (depreciation improving the net position via FC-denominated assets) is entirely missing — but a second-round consequence of the no-NFA-stock gap.
- ~~**Exogenous, self-contained foreign block**~~ *(verified=false — dropped)*. An exogenous/self-mean-reverting foreign block is **standard and adequate** for a small-open-economy semi-structural model and is shared with ECB-MC, ECB-(RE)BASE and FR-BDF. The only genuine residual is the internal-consistency point that `WPCOM` does not depend on world demand `WY` (captured as a recommendation below), *not* a foreign-block-feedback deficiency.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **no current-account/CAD reporting variable** even as a passive post-solve identity (the cheapest possible win — expose `NX−NM` and a CA proxy, exactly as the catalogue already exposes `TOT`); (ii) **`REWI` is mechanically tied to `RTWI` one-for-one** (L604), so the export-competitiveness basket and the trade basket are assumed to have identical real-FX dynamics, with no separate competitor-price/trading-partner structure; (iii) the **export deflators `PXM/PXS/PXO` close largely to domestic `PC/PM`** rather than world prices (limited pricing-to-market) while `PXRE/PXAG` are world-price-takers; (iv) **world demand `WY` is a single US-proxy AR(1)** with no trading-partner weighting — a real limitation given Australia's Asian/Chinese concentration.

**Recommendations.**

### 4.7 Labour market

**MARTIN.** A labour-demand ECM plus Okun's law, with no search-and-matching, gross flows, vacancies, or endogenous participation. The labour force is residual; participation has no feedback; hours are a trend; NAIRU is an offline random walk.

**Best peers.** AUS-M is by far the richest (Beveridge curve with a vacancy stock, a 7-cohort LTU model driving an endogenous two-component NAIRU, cyclical hours, demographic participation). EMMA adds cyclical hours and a cyclical encouraged-worker participation term, and ties employment to a CES FOC. ECB-MC/BASE have forward-looking labour-demand PAC, endogenous participation, separate cyclical hours, and joint gap/NAIRU determination (WAPRO). FR-BDF has an FOC-based employment target with explicit labour hoarding. The RBA DSGE is deliberately *thinner* on the unemployment margin (no unemployment/NAIRU at all) — a different philosophy.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No vacancies, no Beveridge curve, no gross worker flows** *(high)*. Tightness is the single scalar `LURGAP`. In the 2021–23 record-V/U episode, a single-`LURGAP` model under-states effective tightness and cannot diagnose demand-strength vs deteriorating matching efficiency. **The single largest labour-market gap for inflation forecasting.**
- **Endogenous participation and cyclical hours are absent; the labour force is backed out residually** *(high)*. Procyclical participation is a quantitatively important supply buffer; forcing all adjustment through `LE`/`LUR` over-predicts the unemployment response when participation is moving, biasing the inferred `LURGAP`. The missing cyclical-hours margin mis-times unit-labour-cost adjustment.
- **No hysteresis / endogenous NAIRU response to the cycle** *(medium)*. `TLUR` is an offline random walk; the in-model error-correction is **effectively disabled over the forecast** (see missed gap below). A deep recession or extended tightness cannot move equilibrium unemployment within a forecast.
- **Employment is a reduced-form factor-demand ECM, not a marginal-product FOC, and is purely backward-looking (no labour hoarding)** *(medium)*. Factor-substitution responses rest on free ECM coefficients; MARTIN over-predicts near-term employment falls at the onset of a transitory slowdown.
- **No worker or firm heterogeneity** *(low)*. Single representative aggregate; mainly limits interpretation/decomposition. Appropriately down-weighted given MARTIN's aggregate purpose.
- **Wage determination is a reduced-form Phillips curve, not a bargaining model, and is backward-looking** *(medium)*. No Nash bargain, no minimum-wage/award channel, no forward wage term; wage scenarios driven by expectations re-anchoring or Fair Work decisions must be hand-imposed. (Overlaps prices_wages.)

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **the `LUR`→NAIRU error-correction is effectively disabled** because `LUR_DUM = 0` until 2031Q4 — `LUR` has essentially *no equilibrium level anchor* in a normal-horizon solve; (ii) `TY` and `LOKLAG` are themselves **offline Kalman random-walk states**, so the entire Okun mapping rests on offline objects that cannot adjust within a scenario; (iii) **no after-tax-wage effect on labour supply** anywhere — blocking any participation/hours response to income-tax/transfer scenarios (relevant to the thin fiscal block); (iv) the wage-unemployment loop's gain rests on **freely-estimated single-equation coefficients** with no bargaining/efficiency-wage micro-foundation closing it.

**Recommendations.**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | **Add a vacancy stock and Beveridge curve** (ABS Job Vacancies 6354.0) and a tightness composite loading on both `LURGAP` and a vacancy-gap, feeding `PW`/`PTM`/`NBRSP`. **Note the 6354.0 collection suspension (2008Q3–2009Q3) needs a splice**, and any `PW/PTM` coefficient change must be re-baselined against the frozen reference. | AUS-M `nveq`; ECB-MC tightness-in-Phillips-curve | medium | high | needs new data |
| L2 | **Endogenise participation** with a cyclical encouraged/discouraged-worker term and **flip the labour-force closure** to `LF = LPOP*LPR/100`. A system change requiring re-estimation and a re-baseline (but no forward solver). | ECB-MC/BASE participation ECM; EMMA | medium | high | needs system re-estimation |
| L3 | **Add a cyclical average-hours equation** (`DLOG(LHPP)` error-correcting to the trend but loading on the output gap). *Where MARTIN's simplicity is defensible:* the single-Okun spine and aggregate NAIRU are coherent for an aggregate forecast, and worker heterogeneity is appropriately low priority. The structurally-faithful priorities are the **vacancy/Beveridge tightness measure (L1)** and **cyclical participation + hours (L2, L3)** — and, if multiple closure-changing recs are adopted, the labour-block causal ordering should be **redesigned once**, not re-baselined twice.

### 4.8 Inflation, wages & the price system

**MARTIN.** Single-equation ECM Phillips curves with adaptive expectations plus a large deflator cascade. Core `PTM` is a hybrid gap-plus-marginal-cost curve with a **single linear** `LURGAP`; wages `PW` are a separate curve; both impose a vertical long-run restriction. Explicit multi-stage exchange-rate pass-through. Oil enters only at headline.

**Best peers.** The RBA DSGE (forward, marginal-cost NKPCs with estimated flat slopes; mark-up shocks dominate CPI variance), AUS-M (asymmetric wage response via `pos()`×union density; a supply-to-demand price system on the production structure), EMMA (state-space NAIRU wage curve with a 1993 break and explicit hysteresis; cost-of-production price system), ECB-BASE/MC and ECB-(RE)BASE (forward+backward marginal-cost NKPCs; a **time-varying inflation attractor**; an explicit energy-import term in the core curve), FR-BDF (VA-price keystone; input-output deflator weights; **re-estimated slopes through the COVID/energy episode**). The Bulletin validates MARTIN's explicit `PM→PMCG→PTM` chain as the right place to invest.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No convexity / state-dependence in the Phillips slope** *(high)*. Both curves enter the cycle linearly (`PTM` has no scaling at all; `PW`'s `LURGAP/LUR` is a mild denominator effect). A linear MARTIN under-predicts the inflation pickup in a hot economy and over-predicts disinflation as slack returns. **The single most consequential gap on this dimension** given convexity is now a first-order empirical fact.
- **No downward nominal wage rigidity / asymmetric wage response** *(medium)*. `PW` is fully symmetric. *Both Australian peers do better* (AUS-M's `pos()`×union-rate term — verified verbatim in the AUS-M equation listing; EMMA's hysteresis). MARTIN over-predicts wage disinflation/deflation in recessions.
- **Fixed inflation anchor: `PI_E` cannot de-anchor or over-anchor** *(high)*. The ECB family uses a time-varying attractor whose law of motion feeds back from realised inflation; MARTIN always pulls to 2.5%. *(Verifier precision: `PI_E` is an **in-model** appended equation solved each period — its law of motion simply does not reference solved inflation. This makes the recommended fix feasible.)*
- **No forward-looking expectations term in any Phillips curve** *(medium)*. No `E_t π_{t+1}`; MARTIN mis-times the response to anticipated policy and pre-announced cost shocks. Defensibly out of scope for a backward solver.
- **Oil/energy enters only at headline, not in the core curve** *(medium)*. Energy reaches `PTM` only indirectly and slowly via the lagged `PMCG` level — under-capturing the 2022–23 energy-into-core dynamics.
- **Phillips-curve slope frozen at the 2019Q3 sample** *(medium)*. The slope used in 2026 forecasts is conditioned on a sample that omits the very episode policymakers care about; FR-BDF found material slope changes (wage flatter, price steeper).
- **Wage-price spiral anchored only by exogenous `PI_E`, so within-solve second-round dynamics are muted** *(medium)*. A cost-push shock raising `PTM` does not strongly raise expected inflation and feed back to wages within the forecast.
- **Deflator system uses hard-coded unit-cointegration restrictions rather than input-output / expenditure weights** *(low)*. Relative-price responses are less disciplined by trade structure than the ECB/FR-BDF input-output approach.
- **Exchange-rate pass-through is constant, not state-dependent or invoice-currency-aware** *(low)*. Since pass-through carries most of the inflation action, a constant elasticity mis-sizes the inflation consequence of a large AUD depreciation in a high-inflation state.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) a **quarterly-vs-year-ended timing mismatch** — `PTM` is modelled in quarterly log-differences with a contemporaneous gap while policy uses year-ended inflation, giving thin intrinsic year-ended persistence; (ii) a **pure markup-over-marginal-cost long run with no time-varying or cyclical desired markup and no markup/profit-margin shock** — yet the RBA's own DSGE finds mark-up shocks explain ~76% of CPI variance, and 2021–23 featured margin-led price increases (an empirically first-order omission); (iii) an **identification/circularity caveat** — `PEX` is constructed to track `PTM`, so the markup-over-marginal-cost target is imposed by construction rather than identified from an independent price series; (iv) **no pandemic/supply-bottleneck or relative-price-shock term** — discrete price-level events rely on hand-set historical dummies with no forward analogue.

**Recommendations.**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **Add a convex / state-dependent gap term** to `PTM` (and an asymmetric change-in-unemployment term to `PW`): `... + c7*LURGAP + c8*LURGAP*|LURGAP|`. ### 4.9 Stock-flow consistency & balance sheets

**MARTIN.** Partial and asymmetric: capital stocks and the household balance sheet accumulate (the household real-asset side with a genuine housing-valuation channel), but no government-debt, NFA, corporate or banking stocks, no income side of GDP, and no Godley adding-up constraint.

**Best peers.** EMMA (a four-agent flow-of-funds matrix where net lending sums to zero every period — the most directly relevant) and AUS-M (substantially SFC, full external and government stock accounting with valuation effects) are the Australian benchmarks. FR-BDF (five-sector SFC with explicit revaluation), ECB-BASE/MC (household net-worth accumulation with a revaluation term; government debt; NFA feeding consumption). The RBA DSGE is comparable-partial (wins on capital-into-supply and the NFA loop; MARTIN wins on the explicit household balance sheet).

**Verified gaps.**

- **No government budget constraint and no public-debt stock** *(high)*. The single largest stock-flow hole; any fiscal scenario is mechanically demand-neutral in the long run. *(Shared with §4.5; addressed by G1/G3.)*
- **No net-foreign-asset stock and no balance-of-payments closure** *(high)*. The current account simply does not close; the exchange rate has no anchor in the external stock position. *(Shared with §4.6; addressed by X1/X3.)*
- **Household financial wealth `NHFA` is an ECM to income, not an accumulated saving-plus-revaluation stock; the saving flow `NHS` is a dead end** *(high)*. No equity-wealth → consumption channel; saving-rate shocks don't cumulate; the household sheet is not internally accounting-consistent (a poor basis for HANK-style extension). **The highest-leverage gap here — it repairs the one sector MARTIN already half-models.**
- **No corporate or banking/financial-intermediary balance sheet** *(medium)*. No balance-sheet-driven financial accelerator on the firm side. Genuinely hard in a single-equation ECM; only FR-BDF among peers fully accumulates the corporate sheet.
- **No closed transactions-and-revaluation (Godley) matrix; net-lending positions not constrained to sum to zero** *(medium)*. Sectoral imbalances are undisciplined; the system is locally but not globally SFC. Cost mostly to medium-run scenario coherence.
- **Capital stocks accumulate but never enter potential output** *(medium)*. A stock-into-flow failure; cross-listed with §4.2.
- **Depreciation rates are exogenous random walks** *(low)*. Every capital-stock level inherits whatever depreciation is imposed; a defensible simplification.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **no income-side / production-side build of GDP at all** — nominal GDP is constructed *only* from the expenditure side, which is the **root reason** a corporate sector cannot be built (no profit/retained-earnings flow), MARTIN cannot do the AUS-M "Sudoku," and several recommendations silently presuppose an income side that does not exist; (ii) **inventories (`KV/V`) carry no holding-gain/valuation flow**; (iii) even the housing-valuation channel revalues `NHNFA` through a mechanical unit-elasticity product with no separate capital-gains accounting (so the "accounting-consistent" real-asset side is itself a behavioural approximation); (iv) the `KIBRE(-6)→XRE` link — MARTIN's only capital-into-flow-beyond-investment channel — is a single lagged level term, not a production relation, and should not be cited as a mitigant.

**Recommendations.** (The cross-listed structural recs are G1/G3 for the government sheet and X1/X3 for the external sheet; the genuinely SFC-specific recs:)

### 4.10 Stochastic structure, shocks & uncertainty

**MARTIN.** Deterministic Gauss-Seidel point solve; add-factors are a residual-handover device (decayed `*-0.5`); `run_sims.prg`/`sensitivity_matrix()` is a one-at-a-time IRF generator. SIBYL adds an opt-in disturbance band that ignores parameter and cross-equation-correlation uncertainty.

**Best peers.** The RBA DSGE (~20–30 identified structural shocks; variance and historical decompositions; posterior IRF bands) is the sharpest in-house contrast. ECB-BASE supports stochastic simulation/fan charts and a **principled state-space residual model** with estimated persistence. ECB-MC has the BME standardised-shock framework and the **formal forecast/judgement decomposition**. ECB-(RE)BASE does residual-inversion-then-replay-by-family. EMMA, AUS-M, FR-BDF and the Bulletin are broadly *level* with MARTIN on stochastics (all run deterministic conditional solves for the actual forecast) — so a deterministic core is the **standard, defensible mode**.

**Verified gaps.**

- **No native forecast-uncertainty quantification; the SIBYL band ignores parameter and cross-equation correlation** *(high)*. Independent normals over only the forecast tail → bands systematically too narrow and mis-shaped, especially for persistent variables. A forecast round cannot honestly communicate risk.
- **No structural-shock identification and no historical shock decomposition** *(high)*. MARTIN's residuals are never orthogonalised; a miss cannot be decomposed into interpretable drivers. *Honest caveat:* a stitched single-equation ECM cannot identify shocks as cleanly as a DSGE — the achievable target is a **residual-family** decomposition, not full structural identification.
- **Add-factor decay is mechanical and economically wrong as a judgement tail; no estimated residual persistence** *(medium)*. The uniform sign-flipping `*-0.5` is defensible for *historical residual handover* but wrong for sustained forecaster judgement (the LUR-carry incident is the symptom). Tails are hand-chosen, not data-driven.
- **No formal forecast / judgement decomposition accounting** *(medium)*. No split into data-revision / carry-over / new-assumptions / implied-judgement; the raw materials (add-factor metadata, re-solve differencing) exist.
- **Alternative-closure / scenario machinery is ad hoc rather than a named, governed catalogue** *(low)*. The `exogenize` lever exists and is tested; what is missing is packaging and governance.
- **Deterministic point-solve as the default is defensible — not every absence is a true gap** *(low, an anti-gap)*. The gap is the *absence of an uncertainty/decomposition layer*, **not** the deterministic core, which is the standard mode and whose replacement would break the reproducibility-against-bimets guarantee.

*Corrected gap:* the "no analytic multiplier/IRF matrix and no standardised shock library" gap is **narrowed** — the per-equation shock→aggregate IRF engine already exists as the tested `sensitivity_matrix()`. The true residual gaps are (a) **no library of named economic shocks** (monetary/FX/world-demand/fiscal at standard sizes) and (b) **bimets `MULTMATRIX` is unused**.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **unused bimets `RENORM`** (targeting/instrument-inversion) — the architecture-native way to deliver disciplined conditioning-to-a-path and back out the add-factors needed to hit a target, the capability the shock-identification gap says is missing; (ii) **no uncertainty on the offline Kalman states** (`TLUR/NAIRU`, the trends, `PI_E`, `RSTAR`) — first-order for medium-term fan width and never perturbed; (iii) **asymmetric/bounded-variable mis-shaping** — the ELB floor makes a symmetric Gaussian band wrong-shaped near the lower bound, exactly where fan-chart honesty matters most; (iv) **no backtesting / empirical calibration** of the band against realised forecast errors.

**Recommendations.**

| # | Change | Exemplar | Effort | Payoff | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| U1 | **Upgrade the stochastic band to a residual-bootstrap with cross-equation correlation** (block-bootstrap of joint residual vectors, or draws from `N(0, Σ̂)`), over the **whole** horizon, with an optional parameter-uncertainty outer loop (redraw coefficients from each equation's vcov). ### 4.11 Estimation, identification & methodology

**MARTIN.** Single-equation, classical, equation-by-equation. Identification by theory-imposed restrictions, not instruments/sign restrictions. Every behavioural sample ends 2019Q3; the SIBYL default re-fits free coefficients on that embedded sample. Offline trends are quasi-Bayesian Kalman state spaces whose uncertainty is discarded online. No real-time/vintage handling, no formal coefficient-stability testing, structural breaks absorbed by hand-placed dummies.

**Best peers.** The RBA DSGE and ECB-MC (full-information / Bayesian system estimation, with priors compensating for short spans), ECB-BASE/(RE)BASE (a Bayesian WAPRO core jointly identifying the gap and NAIRU), AUS-M (extensive joint/systems estimation under cross-equation FOC/AIDS restrictions), FR-BDF (block-local PAC with a Bayesian VAR core; a **re-estimation policy** that explicitly assesses stability against 2019 and reconstructs residuals across base-year revisions). EMMA is the methodological twin (single-equation ML, classical) and explicitly argues this "places more weight on the data."

**Verified gaps.**

- **No real-time / point-in-time (vintage) estimation database** *(high)*. Coefficients and trends are fit on final-revised data; the live forecast is conditioned on a jump-off where the latest 1–3 quarters will be revised. SIBYL's `vintage` column is download-stamping, not point-in-time reconstruction. The add-factor handover (fitted+af=actual) is pinned to first-print data that will move, so the same round is **not reproducible across vintages** — exactly the failure vintage-tracking is meant to prevent but does not yet close.
- **Default-frozen 2019Q3 sample is increasingly stale; no formal coefficient-stability monitoring** *(high)*. The live 2026 model runs slopes fit ~6.5 years and one regime before the jump-off; there is **no recursive/CUSUM/Quandt-Andrews/Chow test anywhere** (grep-confirmed), so drift is silent. Frozen-by-default is defensible for reproducibility; *having no diagnostic to tell the EA when frozen has become wrong* is the real gap.
- **Trend-uncertainty (NAIRU, r*, potential, `PI_E`) is discarded after offline estimation** *(high)*. The state-space SEs never propagate into the forecast (and the stochastic band perturbs equation disturbances, not the trend states). The output gap drives the entire inflation block and the rule; treating these latent states as known understates uncertainty exactly where it is largest.
- **Two-step offline/online split breaks cross-equation feedback in identification** *(medium)*. The NAIRU that best fits inflation depends on the Phillips slope, which is estimated conditional on the NAIRU — never reconciled jointly (unlike WAPRO). Can bias the slope and the NAIRU in offsetting ways.
- **Almost no instrumenting for simultaneity despite contemporaneous regressors throughout — only `LE` is TSLS** *(medium)*. 42 OLS equations include contemporaneous endogenous regressors (deflators on `D(LOG(PTM))`/`D(LOG(PM))`; `M` on `D(LOG(IAD))`); simultaneity bias feeds the inflation and external blocks. Defensible as a simplicity choice, but a genuine econometric cost.
- **No nowcasting / mixed-frequency bridge inside the handover layer** *(medium)*. The `nowcast` package is "deliberately simple"; jump-off nowcast quality largely determines the first 2–4 quarters — the horizon policymakers care about most. A SIBYL-layer gap (MARTIN being quarterly-structural is appropriately not faulted).
- **Identifying restrictions imposed by hand, never formally tested or reported** *(low)*. No LR/Wald test of any restriction (is long-run pass-through actually 1?); the EA cannot distinguish a data-consistent restriction from a rejected one. Most are economically well-motivated (BGP neutrality), so a transparency gap.
- **Re-estimation, when invoked, re-fits free coefficients but cannot re-identify the restrictions or the offline trends jointly** *(medium)*. `coefficients="reestimated"` rewrites the `TSRANGE` end and re-runs `ESTIMATE`, but keeps the `RESTRICT` structure and does **not** re-run the offline state spaces — so a "re-estimated" MARTIN pairs new Phillips/Okun slopes with a **stale NAIRU/potential**, with no warning. This undermines the one escape hatch from the frozen default.

*Folded-in missed gaps:* (i) **sample-period heterogeneity** — equation start years span 1959q3–1998q1, so cross-equation restrictions and identities bind coefficients that **never saw a common information set**; (ii) a **generated-regressor / two-step SE problem** — smoothed latent trends (`TDLLA`, `TLUR`, `PI_E`, …) are inserted as if observed on the RHS of behavioural equations without a Murphy–Topel correction, biasing the behavioural standard errors independently of simultaneity; (iii) **RSTAR double-counting** — `rstar.prg` contains its *own* parallel output-gap/Okun/Phillips structure that need not agree with the in-model `PTM/LUR` or the separately-estimated NAIRU, so MARTIN effectively carries *two inconsistent Phillips-curve/gap estimates*; (iv) **`PI_E`'s in-model law is a frozen calibration** (0.9/0.1/2.5) immune even to `coefficients="reestimated"`, despite being a first-order driver of every Phillips curve.

**Recommendations.**

## 5. Cross-cutting themes

Five structural threads recur across the eleven dimensions. Each is a single root cause behind several listed gaps, and tackling each unlocks a cluster of capabilities.

**Theme 1 — The absence of forward-looking expectations and a forward solver.** This single fact drives the headline gaps in *expectations* (no MCE, no anticipated news), *monetary policy* (no forward yield curve, muted expectations channel), *external* (no forward UIP, no FX overshoot-on-news), *prices/wages* (no `E_t π_{t+1}`, no credibility channel), and *labour* (no labour hoarding), and it bounds what *uncertainty* can do (no expectation-regime scenarios). Crucially, **two distinct sub-problems are conflated and should be separated**: (a) the *autonomous adaptive anchor* `PI_E`, which is fixable *within the backward architecture* by making it endogenous to realised inflation (E1/P3) — cheap and high-payoff; and (b) the *forward solve* (MCE, news shocks, forward UIP, a forward yield curve), which genuinely requires a stacked-time/Fair-Taylor solver layered on bimets in a two-block fixed point with the backward core — a major research track. **Tackling (a) unlocks de-anchoring/re-anchoring analysis and a live wage-price spiral immediately. Tackling (b) is the one change that would close forward guidance, anticipated-news, and FX-overshoot in one stroke** — but it is correctly a separate, scoped programme, not a forecast-round feature, and it must remain an explicit opt-in mode (parallel to `MARTINMOD_EST.txt`) so the frozen default stays bit-stable.

**Theme 2 — The labour-only supply side.** No production function, no capital in potential, no TFP, no capacity utilisation. This drives the *supply* gaps directly, the *fiscal* gap that public investment cannot raise capacity, the *stock-flow* gap that accumulated capital never feeds the flow it should, and the *external* limitation that mining capital touches output only through one reduced-form lag. The deeper, under-appreciated cost is **internal inconsistency**: MARTIN filters *three separate capacity objects* (the NAIRU in `nairu.prg`, the ygap in `rstar.prg`, the in-solve Okun `LOKLAG/TY`) that need not agree, and the model's entire nominal anchor rests on two backward objects (an autonomous `PI_E` and a backward year-ended policy rule). Adding a Cobb-Douglas potential identity (S1) plus a parallel output-gap diagnostic (S2/S6) would give the inflation block a second, capital-and-output-based read on capacity and surface Okun-relationship drift — unlocking coherent answers to the capex-cycle and durable-vs-transient-productivity questions that dominate the current Australian debate.

**Theme 3 — Thin fiscal and external stock accounting (the missing balance sheets).** MARTIN accumulates physical capital and the household sheet but has **no government-debt stock, no net-foreign-asset stock, no corporate/banking sheet, and no income side of GDP**, so it cannot enforce a flow-of-funds adding-up constraint. This is one root cause behind the *fiscal*, *external*, and *stock-flow* dimensions simultaneously. The good news, established repeatedly above, is that **these are backward-looking accounting identities that the existing Gauss-Seidel solver handles natively** — they are structurally identical to the perpetual-inventory and household-balance-sheet blocks already present. Bolting on a government budget-and-debt layer (G1), an NFA-stock + current-account closure (X1), a debt-elastic FX premium (X3), and repairing the household financial-wealth law of motion (SF1) would, together, give MARTIN a near-complete sectoral balance sheet, unlock fiscal-sustainability and external-sustainability scenarios, close the GNI-vs-GDP wedge, and — via the saving→wealth→consumption and NFL→FX loops — add genuine stabilising feedback the model currently lacks. The binding prerequisite for the corporate-sheet pieces is an **income side of GDP** (SF3), which several recommendations silently assume.

**Theme 4 — Reduced-form everywhere, and the reproducibility hazards that come with it.** MARTIN's equations are individually-estimated reduced forms rather than FOCs of a common optimisation, which is a legitimate, transparent, data-weighted design — but it has two costs the review keeps surfacing. First, *identification fragility*: theory-imposed restrictions are never tested (D5), the offline/online split breaks cross-equation feedback (and creates a generated-regressor SE problem and the RSTAR double-Phillips-curve inconsistency), and almost nothing is instrumented (D6). Second, and more operationally urgent, *artefact divergence*: the EViews `.prg`, the bimets `.txt` SIBYL solves, and SIBYL's own LLM-facing catalogue **disagree** on the yield curve and the ELB floor, and no regression test pins either. Because the catalogue is the artefact the judgement layer reads to propose add-factors, this is a *live mis-instruction to the LLM*, not a cosmetic doc bug. **Reconciling and pinning these (M1, E2) is near-zero-cost and should precede all modelling work** — it is a direct application of the project's own reproducibility-against-the-references principle.

**Theme 5 — Honest uncertainty is under-served, but the deterministic core is not the problem.** Across dimensions the recurring uncertainty failures are: trend-state uncertainty discarded at the offline boundary (estimation dimension), a disturbance band that ignores correlation/parameter/bounded-variable structure (uncertainty dimension), no shock decomposition, no judgement accounting, and no frozen-drift diagnostic. None of these requires touching the deterministic Gauss-Seidel solve — which is the *standard and defensible* mode for every semi-structural peer. The unlock is an **uncertainty-and-diagnostics layer**: a correlated-bootstrap fan chart that also draws the Kalman trend states (U1 + D3), a residual-family historical decomposition (U2), a forecast/judgement decomposition (U6), a frozen-drift monitor (D2), and an empirical backtest of the band. Together these would let a forecast round communicate risk honestly and show exactly how much of a projection is model vs assumption vs human judgement — the governance question SIBYL's human-in-the-loop design exists to answer.

## 7. What should NOT change

MARTIN is a central-bank forecast-round tool, and several of its "limitations" are precisely the right calls for that job. Changing them would degrade the model.

1. **The deterministic Gauss-Seidel core as the default solve.** This is the standard operating mode of *every* semi-structural peer (FR-BDF, ECB-MC, EMMA, AUS-M all run deterministic conditional solves for the actual forecast). It is transparent, fast, robust, and reproducible. All uncertainty/forward-looking capability should be added as **optional layers/modes around it**, never as a replacement — replacing it would break the reproducibility-against-bimets guarantee (design principle 6).

2. **Frozen coefficients (the 2019Q3 published sample) as the default.** Frozen reproduces the published model and guarantees round-to-round, vintage-to-vintage stability. Re-estimation across the COVID break is correctly an explicit opt-in. The fix is a **drift diagnostic that tells the EA when frozen has become wrong** (D2), not abandoning frozen-by-default.

3. **Single-equation, classical, theory-restricted estimation.** Estimating equation-by-equation with imposed homogeneity/BGP-neutrality restrictions is a coherent, data-weighted, transparent methodology that EMMA explicitly defends over Bayesian FIML. It keeps each equation interpretable and debuggable. Add tests and instrumentation as *diagnostics/opt-in variants* (D5, D6), don't convert the model to system FIML.

4. **Backward-looking consumption and investment for the central forecast.** Keeping `RC` as a propensity-to-consume ECM (not an Euler) and investment on cost-of-capital levels is a positive robustness choice — EMMA makes the same call deliberately, and it avoids the forward-guidance puzzle. The forward content belongs at the term structure and FX (where the peers concentrate it), *if and when* a forward solver exists — not in the core demand equations.

5. **A reduced-form capacity gap (the unemployment gap) as the primary inflation/policy gap.** The RBA communicates in unemployment-gap terms, and the Bulletin's own supply-demand framing matches MARTIN's mechanism. A production function should *augment* this with a capital-based cross-check (S2), not *replace* the unemployment-gap spine.

6. **No banking sector / no default block in the core.** This is a deliberate, well-understood scope choice — the RBA maintains the Brassil–Major–Rickards banking-augmented MARTIN separately. The core forecasting model should stay lean; a credit-supply satellite (F5) belongs as an optional, switchable add-on, off by default.

7. **An exogenous, self-mean-reverting foreign block.** Standard and entirely adequate for a small open economy; the RBA cannot move the world. (The only worthwhile refinement is the *internal-consistency* `WPCOM`↔`WY` link, X6 — not endogenising the foreign economy.)

8. **The LLM-as-translator boundary, and human-in-the-loop on add-factors.** Nothing in this review argues for the LLM forecasting. Every forward-guidance/terminal-rate input (M6) is explicitly a *human/market-supplied* input the model propagates; the judgement layer stays a translator and drafter, and numerical add-factors continue to pass through an explicit human-approval gate. The recommendations *strengthen* this discipline — a forecast/judgement decomposition (U6) and a mechanical-audit-style fidelity check make the model-vs-assumption-vs-human split *visible*, which is the point.

**In sum:** MARTIN's design is right for what it is — a transparent, robust, conditional forecasting workhorse with clean judgement entry. The highest-value improvements are not a re-architecture but a set of *backward-compatible accounting layers, single-equation enrichments, an honest uncertainty/diagnostics layer, and (above all) the immediate reconciliation of the model file, the EViews reference, and the LLM-facing catalogue*. The one genuinely transformational structural investment — a forward-looking solver — should be pursued deliberately as a separate, opt-in research track, precisely so that the default model the RBA relies on each round stays exactly as dependable as it is today.
